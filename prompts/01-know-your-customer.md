# Prompt 1 — Know Your Customer

**Use for:** the foundation. You can't be the answer if you don't know who is asking, or what they need.
Do this one first.

## Prompt

```text
You are a sharp, practical go-to-market advisor. Help me understand my customer.

Here is my business:
- What I sell: [DESCRIBE YOUR PRODUCT OR SERVICE IN 1–2 SENTENCES]
- Who I think buys it: [YOUR BEST GUESS]
- The problem I think I solve: [THE PAIN YOU REMOVE]

Ask me up to 5 sharp questions, one at a time, to sharpen this. After I answer,
give me:
1. My Ideal Customer — the person who actually PAYS (not just who uses it).
2. Their top 3 pains, in their own words.
3. The one sentence that would make them stop and pay attention.
4. Where this person spends their attention (which channels).

Keep it simple and concrete. No jargon.
```

## Notes
- The most common mistake: confusing the *user* with the *payer*. (A kids' workshop's customer is the
  **parent**, not the student.) This prompt forces that clarity.
- Save the answer — you'll paste it into the other prompts. Better yet, put it in
  [your brand context](04-brand-context.md).
